Our Hackthon at a glance

Hackly is a week-long event where participants from around the globe work together to build innovative technology projects. There are no teams, everyone is free to share, improve, and build on each other's ideas. The best contributors earn rewards.

Inspired by Wikipedia, The Polymath Project and open source software, it aims to give the crowd the space and incentives to create better solutions through collective intelligence.

We believe that the most impactful contributions are those that spark discussion and encourage collaborative development. To support this, we will implement a leaderboard that ranks contributors based on the interactions they generate. The reward money will be distributed to the top 10% of contributors, rather than to specific individual projects.

Similar to how Wikipedia compiles encyclopedic articles or open-source projects manage code files, centralizing community ideas is key. To simplify collaborative management and updates, each project on Hackly has a wiki page that everyone can access and edit, consolidating the community's collective knowledge from problem statements to proposed solutions.

Each project also includes a forum where members can brainstorm, discuss strategies, ask questions, etc. Participants have complete freedom to define the problems they want to solve and determine their solutions.

Unlike traditional crowdsourcing, you don’t need to come up with complete solutions on your own. In particular, questions and partially formed ideas are encouraged and valued as contributions, as they stimulate discussion and help refine the final solution.

All solutions will be open source and freely accessible. This transparency ensures that everyone can benefit from and build upon the shared knowledge.
